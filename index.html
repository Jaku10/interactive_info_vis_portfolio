Zen Sand Clock

Context: This sketch visualizes time as a calming, continuous flow for users who want a softer, more ambient sense of passing minutes.

  • Context: Designed for people seeking a meditative or reflective moment — such as during study breaks, mindfulness sessions, or quiet routines.
  • Design decisions: Hourglass layout: I chose a classic hourglass shape because it naturally communicates downward flow and gradual change without relying on numbers. Soft colors + transparency: The muted palette and semi‑transparent sand create a calm, non‑distracting visual that fits mindfulness contexts. Smooth particle motion: The sand falls at a steady pace to emphasize relaxation rather than precision, aligning with the idea of time as a gentle rhythm.
  • Future work: Add adjustable “sand speed” to let users choose a tempo that matches their mood.

Interactive Sundial Clock

Context: This sketch visualizes the relationship between sunlight and time, helping users understand how shadows encode the hour.

  • Context: Designed for learners, outdoor enthusiasts, or anyone curious about natural timekeeping — useful in educational settings or as an ambient desktop visual.
  • Design decisions: EShadow‑based time: I used a rotating shadow instead of hands to highlight the physical principles behind sundials. Draggable sun interaction: Allowing users to move the sun helps them explore how light direction affects shadow length and angle. Roman numeral ring: The numerals reinforce the historical feel and make the clock readable without breaking the natural aesthetic.
  • Future work:Add seasonal tilt or latitude adjustments to simulate real‑world sundial behavior.

Track Running Timer

Context: This sketch visualizes pacing and countdown timing for runners who want a simple, intuitive way to track interval durations.

  • Context: Designed for athletes, joggers, or anyone training with timed intervals — useful on a laptop, tablet, or projected during workouts.
  • Design decisions: Oval track layout: I chose a classic running‑track shape because runners naturally think in laps and pacing, making the metaphor instantly familiar. Countdown slider: The slider lets users set their interval duration quickly, supporting flexible training routines. Moving runner dot: The animated dot provides a visual sense of progress and pacing, making time feel active rather than abstract.
  • Future work: Add selectable pacing modes (slow, moderate, fast) to simulate different training intensities.
